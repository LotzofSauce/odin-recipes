Marinated grilled pork tenderloin

Description

Succulent pork tenderloin takes on a sensational transformation in this marinated grilled masterpiece. Bathed in a symphony of flavors, the tenderloin is first tenderized with a zesty marinade crafted from a harmonious blend of garlic, honey, soy sauce, and a hint of tangy Dijon mustard. As it grills to perfection, the savory aromas mingle with the sweet notes of caramelization, creating a tantalizing olfactory symphony. Each bite offers a succulent and juicy experience, with the tender meat bursting with a medley of savory and slightly sweet nuances. Paired with a refreshing summer salad or grilled vegetables, this dish is sure to elevate any outdoor dining experience to gourmet heights.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 4 teaspoons minced fresh ginger root
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 (12 ounces) pork tenderloins

Steps

  1. Make marinade: Whisk together honey, soy sauce, oyster sauce, brown sugar, ginger, ketchup, garlic, parsley, onion powder, cayenne pepper, and cinnamon in a medium bowl; pour into a resealable plastic bag.
  2. Place pork tenderloins into the bag; coat with marinade, squeeze out excess air, and seal the bag. Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour or up to 24 hours.
  3. Preheat the grill for medium heat and lightly oil the grate.
  4. Remove pork tenderloins from marinade; shake off excess. Discard remaining marinade.
  5. Cook pork tenderloins on the preheated grill until no longer pink in the center, 20 to 30 minutes, turning occasionally. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the centers should read at least 145 degrees F (63 degrees C).
